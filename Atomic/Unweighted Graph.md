## What is an Unweighted Graph?
An unweighted graph is a graph where edges do not carry numerical weights. All edges are treated as equal, so algorithms focus on structure and connectivity rather than cost.

In practice, an unweighted graph can be seen as a weighted graph where every edge has the same weight (for example, weight $1$). This is why shortest-path problems in unweighted graphs are often solved with BFS.
