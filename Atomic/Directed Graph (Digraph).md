## What is a Directed Graph (Digraph)?
A directed graph (or digraph) is a graph in which every edge has a direction. A directed edge from $u$ to $v$ is written $(u,v)$ and does not imply that $(v,u)$ also exists.

Digraphs are useful for asymmetric relationships such as one-way streets, hyperlinks between web pages, prerequisite structures, or data-flow dependencies.
